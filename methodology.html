Our Methodology

Assessment Methodology

A structured, repeatable process that ensures every engagement is thorough, accurate, and fully documented. We do not rely on scanner output alone.

9-Step Process

How We Work

From scope agreement to retest validation — every engagement follows the same rigorous structure.

01

Scope & NDA

Define assessment boundaries and sign a mutual NDA before any work begins. Your architecture is protected from the first conversation.

02

Asset Mapping

Enumerate all in-scope assets — endpoints, authentication surfaces, API routes, and third-party integrations. Full attack surface documented before testing starts.

03

Automated Discovery

Burp Suite Professional and OWASP ZAP active scanning across all discovered endpoints. Authenticated and unauthenticated sessions tested separately.

04

Manual Verification

Every automated finding manually confirmed by a certified analyst. Business logic flaws, IDOR, and chained attacks that tools cannot detect.

05

Exploitability Analysis

Confirmed vulnerabilities assessed for real-world exploitability — attack complexity, privilege requirements, and potential business impact.

06

CVSS Risk Scoring

Every finding scored using CVSS v3.1 — Base Score, Vector String, exploitability and impact metrics. Prioritised by real-world risk.

07

Executive & Technical Reporting

One report, two audiences. Executive summary for leadership. Full technical appendix with proof-of-concept evidence for your development team.

08

Remediation Guidance

Practical, developer-friendly fix steps for every finding. Prioritised roadmap so your team addresses critical issues first.

09

Retest Validation

After fixes are applied, we retest targeted findings to confirm vulnerabilities have been properly resolved. Documented in a retest report.

We do not rely on scanner output alone. Findings are reviewed, validated, and documented with actionable evidence. Every critical finding includes a proof-of-concept and a clear remediation path before the report is delivered.
